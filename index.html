Got Questions?

FAQ

" What is the role of a CBE educator?

A CBE educator facilitates learning by guiding learners to acquire and demonstrate competencies. Rather than focusing solely on content delivery, they create engaging learning experiences that help learners develop knowledge, skills, values, and attitudes.

— Teacher's Role
" How does a CBE teacher assess learners?

A CBE teacher uses continuous assessment methods such as observations, projects, discussions, practical activities, portfolios, and performance tasks to evaluate learners' mastery of competencies rather than relying only on examinations.

— Assessment
" What teaching methods are used in Competency-Based Education?

CBE educators use learner-centered approaches such as group discussions, inquiry-based learning, project-based learning, role play, problem-solving activities, and collaborative tasks that encourage active participation and critical thinking.

— Teaching Methods
" How does a CBE teacher promote creativity and innovation?

By providing open-ended tasks, encouraging learners to generate original ideas, and allowing them to explore multiple solutions to real-life challenges, CBE teachers nurture creativity, imagination, and innovation.

— Creativity
" Why is collaboration important in a CBE classroom?

Collaboration helps learners develop teamwork, communication, leadership, and problem-solving skills. CBE teachers regularly organize group activities and projects that encourage learners to work together toward common goals.

— Collaboration
